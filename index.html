Teaching Philosphy

As a TAS teacher with one year of experience at Holy Spirit, my understanding of education has deepened beyond what I ever imagined. Growing up in a family of educators, with both my mother and grandmother being teachers, the passion for teaching feels ingrained in me. My journey began early, working with children in various capacities, from babysitting to teaching dance and running summer camps. I've always been told I have a natural ability to connect with children, making communication seem effortless. However, my experience has taught me that teaching is much more than simply having a love for children and a desire to help them learn. The purpose of education, as I see it, extends beyond just preparing students for citizenship or equipping them with the skills to achieve their dreams. While literacy and critical thinking are essential, I am inspired by the thought of education as a means to unlock students' potential. As Albert Einstein once said, “Education is not the learning of facts, but the training of the mind to think.” This encapsulates my belief that true success in education is when students are empowered to become independent thinkers and lifelong learners. My teaching philosophy is rooted in the belief that every student has the potential to succeed when given the right opportunities. This belief drives me to get to know each of my students personally, understanding their learning styles, interests, and even aspects of their home life. Building these connections allows me to tailor my teaching to engage students in ways that resonate with them. In my classroom, I take time to learn about my students’ hobbies and personal experiences, integrating these into the curriculum to make learning more relevant and meaningful. Open communication with parents is also crucial, and I maintain an open-door policy, ensuring that parents feel involved and supported in their child’s education. I am committed to continuous learning and growth, both for myself and my students. As the philosopher John Dewey said, “Education is not preparation for life; education is life itself.” Teaching requires flexibility and the ability to adapt to the ever-changing dynamics of the classroom. Nothing ever goes exactly as planned, but the ability to remain adaptable and see each challenge as a learning opportunity is what makes a teacher truly effective. In the years to come, I aspire to teach with curiosity, care, and encouragement. My goal is to create a classroom environment where learning is not only about academic achievement but also about personal growth and development. By embodying these values and remaining open to the lessons my students teach me, I believe I can become the best educator I can be..

The study of Industrial Technology provides students with opportunities to engage in a diverse range of creative and practical experiences using a variety of technologies widely available in industrial and domestic settings. This may include study in the focus areas of:

  • Automotive
  • Building and Construction
  • Electronics
  • Engineering
  • Farm Maintenance
  • Metal
  • Multimedia
  • Timber
Students develop knowledge and understanding of materials and processes. Related knowledge and skills are developed through a specialised approach to the tools, materials, equipment and techniques employed in the planning, development, construction and evaluation of quality practical projects and processes. Critical thinking skills are developed through engagement with creative practical problem-solving activities.
